Our Academic Programs

A curriculum built to inspire curiosity, develop strong foundations, and cultivate confident, capable learners from the earliest years.

Academic Philosophy

Excellence Starts With the Right Foundation

At Fahmid School, we believe that every child is capable of extraordinary things when they are taught well, cared for genuinely, and given the right environment to grow.

Our curriculum combines academic rigour with creative exploration, structured learning with imaginative play, and high expectations with warm, personalised support. The result is pupils who are not only academically strong, but confident, curious, and well-rounded individuals.

Why Our Approach Works

Built on Sound Principles

Every element of our academic delivery is intentional, evidence-informed, and centred on the child.

Small Class Sizes

Our low pupil-to-teacher ratio means every child receives meaningful individual attention and no learner falls through the cracks.

Balanced Curriculum

Academic rigour and creative expression are given equal weight, producing pupils who think broadly and perform strongly across all subjects.

Qualified, Dedicated Staff

Our teachers are trained, experienced, and passionate. Professional development is ongoing so our staff continue to grow alongside our pupils.

Proven Track Record

Our pupils consistently achieve excellent results and gain places in strong secondary schools across the region — year after year.

Structured Routine

Children thrive in predictable, well-organised days. Our timetables carefully balance learning, creative activity, play, and rest.

Character Development

Academic success matters deeply, but so does character. We actively nurture integrity, discipline, respect, and compassion in every pupil.

Ages 2 – 5

Program One

Nursery Program

A warm, stimulating environment where toddlers and pre-schoolers begin their lifelong learning journey through play, exploration, and guided discovery.

Our nursery program creates a safe and loving setting where very young children feel secure enough to explore, ask questions, and develop at their own pace. Every activity is purposefully designed to build foundational skills while remaining joyful and age-appropriate.

From their first day, nursery children are welcomed into a structured routine that gives them the confidence and stability they need. Teachers work closely with each child and communicate regularly with parents to ensure continuity of care between home and school.

What Nursery Children Learn

Early Literacy
Letters, sounds, phonics, and simple word recognition through songs and stories
Numeracy Skills
Numbers, counting, shapes, patterns, and basic mathematical concepts
Social Development
Sharing, communication, friendships, and emotional intelligence
Creative Arts
Art, music, drama, and imaginative play to develop creativity and expression
Primary 1 – 6

Program Two

Primary Program

A rigorous and comprehensive curriculum that builds the academic foundations pupils need to excel at secondary school and beyond.

Our primary programme covers the full range of subjects required for a strong, well-rounded education. Lessons are engaging and paced appropriately for each class level, with teachers consistently assessing progress to adapt their approach.

Beyond academics, primary pupils are given opportunities for leadership, competitions, school events, and extracurricular activities that build their confidence and sense of identity. By the time our pupils complete Primary 6, they are well-prepared — academically and personally — for the challenges of secondary education.

English Language
Reading, writing, grammar, vocabulary & oral communication
Mathematics
Number operations, problem-solving, geometry & mental maths
Basic Science
Biology, chemistry & physics through hands-on experiments
Social Studies
History, geography, citizenship & cultural awareness
Creative Arts & Physical Education
Visual arts, music, drama, sports & healthy living

Optional Programme

Madrasah Programme

Our Madrasah Programme is an optional after-school programme offered to Muslim pupils who wish to receive structured Islamic education within the school environment.

Participation is entirely voluntary and does not interfere with any regular school activities. The programme holds Monday to Thursday, from 4:00 pm to 6:00 pm, giving pupils a convenient and structured opportunity to develop their Islamic knowledge alongside their academic studies.

How We Measure Progress

Assessment & Reporting

We use comprehensive, fair assessment methods to monitor every child's progress and provide regular, meaningful feedback to parents.

Continuous Assessment

Daily classroom observations and regular evaluations to track learning progress and identify areas for extra support.

Termly Reports

Detailed report cards three times per year, covering academic performance, behaviour, and personal development in full.

Parent Meetings

Scheduled parent-teacher conferences each term to discuss progress, celebrate achievements, and address any concerns.

Quizzes & Competitions

Regular quizzes and academic competitions reinforce learning, reward effort, and assess understanding in an engaging way.

Policy 01

Admission Policy

Purpose and Scope

Fahmid Nursery and Primary School is committed to providing a fair, transparent, and consistent admissions process for all prospective pupils and their families. This policy applies to all new enrolments at the Nursery and Primary levels and governs the procedures by which applications are assessed and places are offered.

Eligibility

The school admits children between the ages of 2 and 12 years, subject to available places and the following entry requirements:

  • Nursery 1 entry: children must be a minimum of 2 years of age by the first day of the new term.
  • Primary 1 entry: children must be a minimum of 5 years of age and must have completed Nursery or equivalent pre-school education.
  • Mid-year and mid-school transfers are considered on the basis of available places and the child's readiness for the relevant class level.

Application Procedure

  1. Parents or guardians complete the official Application Form, available from the school office or the school website.
  2. Completed forms must be submitted along with the required documentation and the non-refundable application fee.
  3. The school will acknowledge receipt of all applications within five working days.
  4. Applicants will be invited for an informal assessment and parent interview where appropriate.
  5. Successful applicants will receive a formal offer letter and are required to confirm acceptance within ten working days.
  6. A place is not secured until the registration fee and any required deposits have been paid in full.

Required Documentation

  • A completed Application Form signed by a parent or legal guardian.
  • Two recent passport-sized photographs of the child.
  • Certified copy of the child's birth certificate.
  • Immunisation record and health declaration from a registered medical practitioner.
  • Most recent school report (for transfer pupils entering Primary 2 and above).
  • A copy of valid identification for the parent or guardian.

Admissions Criteria

Where the school is oversubscribed, places will be offered in the following order of priority:

  1. Siblings of current pupils already enrolled at the school.
  2. Children of current members of staff.
  3. Returning pupils seeking re-enrolment after a period of absence.
  4. All other applicants, assessed in order of the date on which a complete application was received.

Fahmid School does not discriminate on the grounds of gender, ethnicity, religion, disability, or socioeconomic background in its admissions process. All eligible children are considered on equal terms in accordance with this policy.

Waiting List

Where a year group is full, eligible applicants will be placed on a waiting list in order of application date. Parents on the waiting list will be contacted as soon as a place becomes available.

Policy 02

Attendance Policy

Purpose

Regular attendance is essential to a child's academic progress, social development, and overall wellbeing. This policy sets out the school's expectations, procedures, and responsibilities in relation to pupil attendance and punctuality.

Expectations

The school expects all pupils to attend every scheduled school day unless absence is unavoidable. The school day runs from 7:40 AM to 3:30 PM, and pupils are expected to be present and ready to begin learning at the start of morning assembly. The school targets a minimum attendance rate of 90% for all pupils in each term.

Reporting an Absence

  • If a child is unable to attend school, a parent or guardian must notify the school office by 8:00 AM on the first day of absence, either by telephone or via the parent portal.
  • A written explanation must be provided upon the child's return to school. The class teacher will review and file this note.
  • Absences of three or more consecutive days require a medical certificate or equivalent documentation.
  • Unexplained absences will be recorded as unauthorised until an acceptable reason is provided.

Authorised and Unauthorised Absences

The school will authorise absences in the following circumstances: certified illness, bereavement of an immediate family member, a medical appointment that cannot be scheduled outside school hours, or a religious observance. Holidays taken during term time will not be authorised except in exceptional circumstances agreed in advance with the Head Teacher.

Persistent Absence

Where a pupil's attendance falls below 85% in any given term, the school will initiate a formal review. Parents will be invited to meet with the class teacher or Head Teacher to identify and address the underlying cause. A written Attendance Improvement Plan may be put in place where necessary.

Punctuality

Pupils arriving after the register has been taken will be recorded as late. Persistent lateness is treated with the same seriousness as unauthorised absence and will be raised with parents at the earliest opportunity.

Policy 03

Child Protection & Safeguarding Policy

The safeguarding and welfare of every child in our care is the single highest priority of Fahmid Nursery and Primary School. This policy is reviewed annually and takes precedence over all other school policies.

Statement of Commitment

Fahmid School is committed to creating and maintaining a safe, secure, and nurturing environment for every pupil. All staff, volunteers, and contractors working with children are required to share this commitment and to act swiftly and appropriately in response to any concern relating to a child's welfare.

Designated Safeguarding Lead

The school has a designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL) who is responsible for co-ordinating all child protection activity within the school. The DSL receives regular training and is the first point of contact for any safeguarding concern raised by staff, parents, or pupils. Contact details for the DSL are displayed prominently throughout the school.

Recognising Abuse and Neglect

Abuse may take the form of physical harm, emotional harm, sexual abuse, or neglect. All staff are trained to recognise the indicators of abuse and are expected to report any concern — however minor it may seem — to the Designated Safeguarding Lead without delay. Staff must never attempt to investigate a concern themselves.

Reporting Procedures

  1. Any member of staff who has a concern about a child's welfare must report this to the DSL verbally and in writing on the same day.
  2. The DSL will assess the concern and determine the appropriate course of action, which may include referral to statutory authorities.
  3. All safeguarding concerns and actions taken are recorded securely and confidentially.
  4. Where there is an immediate risk of harm to a child, the DSL will contact the relevant authority without delay.

Safe Recruitment

The school follows strict safe recruitment procedures for all staff and volunteers who work with children. These include identity verification, reference checks, and background screening prior to any individual working unsupervised with pupils.

Confidentiality

Information relating to safeguarding concerns is shared only on a need-to-know basis and in the best interests of the child. However, the duty to protect a child takes precedence over the right to privacy, and confidentiality will not be maintained where doing so would place a child at risk.

Safer Use of Technology

The school takes an active role in educating pupils about online safety. The use of personal mobile devices by pupils on school premises is not permitted. Any online activity undertaken on school equipment is monitored and must comply with the school's acceptable use expectations.

Policy 04

Anti-Bullying Policy

Our Stance

Fahmid School operates a zero-tolerance policy towards bullying of any kind. Every pupil has the right to attend school in an environment that is safe, respectful, and free from intimidation. Bullying behaviour, whether directed at pupils, staff, or any member of the school community, is taken extremely seriously and will be addressed promptly and firmly.

Definition of Bullying

Bullying is defined as any repeated, intentional behaviour that causes physical, emotional, or social harm to an individual. It may take a number of forms, including but not limited to:

  • Physical bullying — hitting, pushing, kicking, or damaging the belongings of others.
  • Verbal bullying — name-calling, teasing, threatening, or the use of offensive language.
  • Social bullying — deliberate exclusion, spreading rumours, or manipulating relationships.
  • Cyberbullying — the use of digital communication to harass, threaten, or humiliate others.
  • Discriminatory bullying — targeting an individual on the basis of race, ethnicity, religion, gender, disability, or any other protected characteristic.

Responsibilities

All members of the school community share a responsibility in preventing and responding to bullying. Staff are expected to intervene whenever they witness or suspect bullying. Pupils are encouraged to report any bullying they experience or witness. Parents are asked to notify the school promptly if they believe their child is involved in a bullying incident, as either the victim or the perpetrator.

Reporting and Response

  1. Any pupil who experiences or witnesses bullying should report it to their class teacher or any trusted adult in the school.
  2. The class teacher will document the incident and refer it to the Head Teacher.
  3. Both the pupil who has been bullied and the pupil responsible for the behaviour will be spoken with separately.
  4. Parents of all parties will be notified and invited to meet with the school.
  5. Appropriate consequences and support measures will be put in place for all pupils involved.

All reports of bullying are taken seriously, regardless of how minor they may appear. No pupil will ever be penalised or disadvantaged for making a good-faith report of bullying behaviour.

Prevention

The school takes an active, proactive approach to bullying prevention through dedicated assemblies, pastoral discussions, and an inclusive school culture that celebrates diversity and mutual respect.

Policy 05

Health & Safety Policy

Purpose

The school has a duty of care to all pupils, staff, and visitors. This policy outlines the arrangements by which Fahmid School meets its obligations to provide a safe and healthy environment for learning and working.

General Responsibilities

The Head Teacher holds overall responsibility for health and safety matters within the school. All staff are required to take reasonable care for their own health and safety and that of others who may be affected by their actions. Pupils are expected to behave in a manner that does not endanger themselves or others.

First Aid

  • At least one trained first aider is present on the school premises at all times during the school day.
  • A fully stocked first aid kit is maintained in the school office and in key locations around the school.
  • All first aid administered to pupils is recorded in the school's incident log.
  • Parents are notified promptly of any incident requiring first aid treatment.

Medical Conditions and Medication

Parents must disclose any known medical condition or allergy when registering their child with the school. Where a pupil requires medication during school hours, parents must provide written authorisation and supply the medication in its original, clearly labelled container. Medication is stored securely and administered only by designated staff members.

Emergency Procedures

The school has documented emergency procedures for fire, evacuation, medical emergencies, and other incidents. Fire drills are conducted at least twice per term. All staff are briefed on emergency procedures as part of their induction and receive regular refresher training. Emergency assembly points are clearly marked on all school premises.

Premises and Equipment Safety

The school carries out regular inspections of all buildings, grounds, and equipment. Any hazard identified is reported and remedied promptly. Pupils are not permitted to access areas that are under maintenance or that have been designated as restricted zones.

Communicable Illness

To protect the health of all pupils and staff, parents are asked to keep children at home if they are suffering from a contagious illness. The school follows current public health guidelines on exclusion periods for common childhood illnesses. The school reserves the right to require the temporary exclusion of any child presenting with symptoms of a contagious illness.

Policy 06

Behaviour & Discipline Policy

Our Approach

Fahmid School believes that a positive, structured, and respectful environment is essential to effective learning. Our approach to behaviour management is built on mutual respect, clear expectations, and the belief that all pupils are capable of excellent conduct. We seek to praise and reward positive behaviour while addressing unacceptable behaviour fairly and consistently.

Expected Standards of Behaviour

All pupils are expected to:

  • Treat fellow pupils, staff, and all members of the school community with respect and courtesy at all times.
  • Follow reasonable instructions given by any member of staff.
  • Take care of school property and the personal belongings of others.
  • Behave responsibly during movement around the school, assemblies, and off-site activities.
  • Maintain honest and honourable conduct in assessments and schoolwork.

Rewards

The school recognises and celebrates positive behaviour and achievement through verbal praise, written commendations, merit awards, Head Teacher certificates, and end-of-term prize giving. We believe that recognition is a powerful motivator and endeavour to acknowledge effort and improvement as much as attainment.

Consequences for Unacceptable Behaviour

Consequences are applied proportionately and consistently. The following framework is used:

  1. Verbal warning from the class teacher, with a clear explanation of why the behaviour is unacceptable.
  2. Removal of a privilege, such as free play time, where the behaviour persists.
  3. A written record of the incident and notification to parents.
  4. A meeting between the parents, the pupil, and the class teacher or Head Teacher.
  5. In cases of serious or persistent misconduct, a formal suspension may be imposed. The decision to suspend a pupil rests with the Head Teacher.

Prohibited Conduct

The following behaviours are treated as serious misconduct and will result in immediate escalation: physical assault of another pupil or a member of staff; verbal abuse, threatening language, or harassment; wilful damage to school property; dishonesty or deliberate deception of staff; and possession of any prohibited item on school premises.

Physical Restraint

Physical restraint will only be used by trained members of staff as a last resort where there is an immediate risk of physical harm to the pupil themselves or to others. Any use of physical restraint will be recorded and parents informed on the same day.

The school does not permit any form of corporal punishment. Discipline is administered with dignity and is always aimed at changing behaviour, never at humiliating the child.

Policy 07

Data Protection & Privacy Policy

Our Commitment

Fahmid School collects and processes personal data relating to pupils, parents, guardians, and staff in order to operate effectively and to provide the best possible educational experience. We are committed to handling all personal data lawfully, fairly, and transparently in accordance with applicable data protection legislation.

What Data We Collect

The school may collect and hold the following categories of personal data:

  • Pupil information: full name, date of birth, contact details, medical information, academic records, attendance data, and disciplinary records.
  • Family information: names and contact details of parents and guardians, emergency contacts, and financial information where applicable.
  • Photographs and video recordings of pupils taken during school activities for educational and communications purposes.
  • Staff information: employment records, qualifications, references, and payroll data.

How We Use Personal Data

Personal data is used solely for the legitimate purposes of the school, including: managing pupil enrolment and progress; communicating with parents and guardians; ensuring the health, safety, and welfare of pupils; complying with statutory obligations; and improving the quality of education provided.

Data Storage and Security

All personal data is stored securely. Physical records are kept in locked filing systems. Digital records are protected by passwords and access restrictions. The school regularly reviews its data storage arrangements to ensure that personal data is kept only as long as necessary.

Sharing of Data

The school does not sell personal data to third parties. Data is shared with external parties only where there is a lawful basis to do so, such as: where required by statutory authority; where it is necessary to protect the safety of a pupil; or where a parent has given explicit consent.

Photographs and Media

The school may photograph or film pupils during school events and activities for use in school communications, newsletters, and the school website. Parents are asked to indicate their consent for the use of their child's image when completing the school's registration documentation. Parents may withdraw this consent at any time by writing to the school.

Parental Rights

Parents and guardians have the right to request access to the personal data held by the school relating to their child. Requests should be made in writing to the school office. The school will respond within 30 days of receipt of a valid request.

Policy 08

Staff Conduct Policy

Purpose

This policy sets out the standards of professional conduct expected of all members of staff at Fahmid Nursery and Primary School. It applies to all teaching and non-teaching staff, whether employed on a permanent, temporary, or voluntary basis. Staff are expected to act in a manner that upholds the reputation of the school and the trust placed in them by pupils, parents, and the wider community.

Professional Standards

All members of staff are expected to:

  • Treat all pupils, parents, colleagues, and visitors with dignity, courtesy, and respect.
  • Maintain appropriate professional boundaries with pupils at all times.
  • Arrive punctually and fulfil all assigned duties in a diligent and conscientious manner.
  • Dress in a professional manner consistent with their role and the school's standards.
  • Maintain confidentiality in respect of all matters relating to pupils, families, and colleagues.
  • Report any concern about pupil welfare or safeguarding without delay.
  • Take responsibility for their own continued professional development.

Social Media and Online Conduct

Staff must not post content online that could bring the school into disrepute, identify individual pupils, or reveal confidential information relating to the school or its community. Staff must not communicate with pupils or their families via personal social media accounts. Any use of the school's official digital platforms must comply with the school's communications guidelines.

Conflicts of Interest

Staff must declare any actual or potential conflict of interest to the Head Teacher at the earliest opportunity. This includes personal or financial relationships with parents, contractors, or other parties that could influence professional judgement or decision-making.

Disciplinary Procedures

Breaches of this policy will be addressed in accordance with the school's disciplinary procedure. Minor breaches may be managed through informal guidance and support. Serious or repeated breaches may result in formal disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment. Any allegation of misconduct involving a child will be referred to the appropriate authority without delay.

Policy 09

Complaints & Grievance Procedure

Our Commitment

Fahmid School welcomes feedback from parents, pupils, and the wider community. We are committed to addressing all complaints fairly, consistently, and within reasonable timeframes. We believe that a robust complaints procedure contributes to continuous improvement and maintains the trust of our school community.

Informal Resolution — Stage 1

In the first instance, concerns should be raised directly with the relevant class teacher or member of staff. Many concerns can be resolved quickly through open and respectful conversation. Parents are encouraged to contact the school as early as possible when a concern arises, rather than allowing it to develop into a more serious complaint.

Formal Complaint — Stage 2

Where a concern cannot be resolved informally, the parent or guardian may submit a formal written complaint to the Head Teacher. The complaint should include:

  • The full name and contact details of the person making the complaint.
  • The name of the pupil to whom the complaint relates.
  • A clear description of the concern, including relevant dates and any previous steps taken to resolve it.
  • The outcome the complainant is seeking.

The Head Teacher will acknowledge receipt of a formal complaint within five working days and will aim to provide a full written response within fifteen working days of the date of acknowledgement.

Escalation — Stage 3

Where a complainant remains dissatisfied following the Head Teacher's response, the matter may be referred to the School Management Board. The Board will review the complaint and the school's response, and will provide a final written determination within twenty working days. The Board's decision is final under the school's internal procedures.

Important Principles

  • All complaints are treated with confidentiality. Only those directly involved in the resolution process will have access to the details of a complaint.
  • No pupil or parent will be disadvantaged as a result of making a genuine complaint in good faith.
  • Anonymous complaints will be considered but the school is under no obligation to investigate where it is not possible to obtain further information.
  • Complaints relating to safeguarding or child protection are handled under the school's separate Safeguarding Policy and will be referred to the appropriate statutory authority where required.

To make a formal complaint, please contact the school office in writing. You may also write to us at: Fahmid Nursery and Primary School, 18 Shittu Street, Adura Bus Stop, Alagbado, Ifako-Ijaiye, Lagos, or by email at info@fahmidschool.ng.
